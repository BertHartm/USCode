§1300l–1. Economic development

(a) Plan for economic development

The Secretary shall—

(1) enter into negotiations with the governing body of the Tribe with respect to establishing a plan for economic development for the Tribe;

(2) in accordance with this section and not later than 2 years after the adoption of a tribal constitution as provided in section 1300l–5 1 of this title, develop such a plan; and

(3) upon the approval of such plan by the governing body of the Tribe, submit such plan to the Congress.

(b) Restrictions

Any proposed transfer of real property contained in the plan developed by the Secretary under subsection (a) of this section shall be consistent with the requirements of section 1300l–2 1 of this title.

(Pub. L. 103–434, title II, §203, Oct. 31, 1994, 108 Stat. 4533.)

References in Text

Section 1300l–5 of this title, referred to in subsec. (a)(2), was in the original “section 107”, which was translated as reading “section 207”, meaning section 207 of Pub. L. 103–434, to reflect the probable intent of Congress, because section 207 of Pub. L. 103–434 relates to adoption of a tribal constitution, but section 107 relates to Yavapai-Prescott Indian Tribe water rights.

Section 1300l–2 of this title, referred to in subsec. (b), was in the original “section 104”, which was translated as reading “section 204”, meaning section 204 of Pub. L. 103–434, to reflect the probable intent of Congress, because section 204 of Pub. L. 103–434 relates to transfer of real property, but section 104 relates to ratification of Yavapai-Prescott Indian Tribe water rights settlement agreement.
